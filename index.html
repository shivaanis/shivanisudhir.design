§ 01

I design apps the way a good editor writes a sentence — spare, exact, and built to be read again tomorrow.

For the last several years I've been the resident designer for Playpal Apps — a small house that ships beautifully crafted utilities, games, and wellness tools for iOS and Android. Ten apps. Two platforms. Every pixel, my responsibility.

My work sits in the small movements: the cadence of a transition, the breath between paragraphs, the colour you didn't notice but felt. I believe restraint is the loudest design choice you can make — and that good products feel inevitable, not invented.

§ 03

The four notes I keep above the desk.

  1. i.

    Restraint is a feature.

    An interface gets better the more I take away. The hardest line to draw is the one that doesn't appear in the final screen.

  2. ii.

    Details are the design.

    A type ramp is not "polish." A spring curve is not "polish." A spacing rhythm is not "polish." They are the design.

  3. iii.

    Pixels are policy.

    Every default tells the user what to do. I treat empty states, error copy, and confirmation moments as the place a product earns trust.

  4. iv.

    Polish, then ship.

    I'd rather hold a release a week than ship a screen that's eighty per cent. Shipping a thing is also the first time you learn what it is.

§ 04

About.

I'm Shivani Sudhir — a product designer working from Sydney. I trained in design, moved into mobile, and have spent the last several years embedded with a small independent studio shipping apps real people pay for with their attention.

I take projects all the way through: research and structure, brand and identity, the full UI system, motion, the App Store screenshots, the marketing site. I prefer to own the surface end-to-end so the seams disappear.

Outside of design I read a lot, drink tea by the litre, and keep a sketchbook I never show anyone.
